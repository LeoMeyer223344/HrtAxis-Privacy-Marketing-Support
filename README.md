HrtAxis Privacy Policy
Effective Date: January 13, 2026
1. Introduction
HrtAxis ("we", "us", "our") operates as a personal health tracking application. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle your information in compliance with applicable laws including the Texas Medical Records Privacy Act (Texas Health & Safety Code Chapter 181), the Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA), and other applicable federal and state privacy laws.
Note on HIPAA: HrtAxis is not a HIPAA-covered entity (it is not a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse) and is not a business associate of any covered entity. Accordingly, HIPAA does not directly govern HrtAxis. However, we voluntarily apply security practices consistent with other app standards as a best practice for protecting health information.
2. Information We Collect
We collect and store the following types of information:

Electrocardiogram (ECG) data from Apple Health
Heart rate measurements from Apple Health
Health metrics including steps, calories, exercise, sleep, blood pressure, and oxygen saturation
Questionnaire responses you provide
Notes and context you add to reports
Photos you attach to reports (with metadata stripped)
Report names and timestamps
Device information for app functionality

3. How We Store Your Data

ALL DATA IS STORED LOCALLY on your device only
We use Apple's secure file protection mechanisms
Data is encrypted at rest using iOS Complete Protection
We do NOT upload, transmit, or store your data on external servers
We do NOT access, view, or process your health information
We do NOT use cloud services or remote storage

4. Data Sharing
We Do NOT Share Your Data:

We do NOT sell your data to third parties
We do NOT share your data with advertisers
We do NOT transmit your data to our servers
We do NOT share data with analytics companies

You Control All Sharing:

You choose when to export reports as PDF
You control who receives exported PDFs
Shared PDFs use iOS standard sharing (outside our control)

5. Your Privacy Rights Under Texas Law
Texas Medical Records Privacy Act (Tex. Health & Safety Code Ch. 181):

Access all your health information
Request correction of inaccurate information
Receive an accounting of disclosures
Request restrictions on use of your information
Receive notice of privacy practices

Texas Data Privacy and Security Act (TDPSA, effective July 1, 2024):
Texas residents have the following rights under the TDPSA:

Right to confirm whether we are processing your personal data and to access that data
Right to correct inaccuracies in your personal data
Right to delete personal data you have provided to us
Right to obtain a portable copy of your personal data in a readily usable format
Right to opt out of the processing of personal data for purposes of targeted advertising, sale of personal data, or profiling (we do none of these)

Because all health data is stored locally on your device and we do not sell or share your health data, most TDPSA processing activities do not apply to us. To exercise any of these rights, contact us at contact@HrtAxis.com. We will respond within 45 days as required by law.
All your health data remains on your device under your exclusive control. You may delete the app at any time to remove all local data.

12. iCloud Backups
If you have iCloud Backup enabled on your device, iOS may include HrtAxis app data (including locally stored health reports) in your iCloud Backup. This backup is encrypted by Apple and subject to Apple's Privacy Policy, not ours. We have no access to iCloud Backup data. To exclude HrtAxis from iCloud Backup, go to iOS Settings → [Your Name] → iCloud → Manage Account Storage → Backups and disable HrtAxis. HrtAxis does not independently sync any data to iCloud or any other cloud service.
13. Data Breach Notification (FTC Health Breach Notification Rule)
HrtAxis is subject to the Federal Trade Commission's Health Breach Notification Rule (16 CFR Part 318), as updated effective July 29, 2024, which covers vendors of personal health records and related applications.
In the event of a breach of security of unsecured personal health record information:

We will notify affected individuals without unreasonable delay and no later than 60 calendar days after discovery of the breach
We will notify the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) no later than 60 calendar days after discovery
If the breach affects 500 or more individuals in a state or jurisdiction, we will notify prominent media outlets in that state or jurisdiction
Notification will include: a description of what happened; the types of information involved; steps affected individuals can take to protect themselves; what we are doing to investigate and mitigate the breach; and contact information for affected individuals to ask questions

Because health data is stored locally on your device, the primary breach risk is unauthorized access to your physical device, which is governed by your device's own security settings. We strongly encourage you to enable device passcode and biometric protection.
